Summary

docs.sui.io recorded 5,051 unique visitors and 15,408 pageviews for April 9–15, 2026, with engagement metrics improving week-over-week. Kapa logged 73 conversations spanning 7 distinct themes, with transaction data fetching, JSON-RPC migration, and unanswered ecosystem questions dominating developer friction.

Plausible

Traffic Overview

docs.sui.io recorded 5,051 unique visitors and 15,408 pageviews over the 7-day period April 9–15, 2026. Engagement is moderate with a 64% bounce rate, 2.7 views per visit, and an average visit duration of 3 minutes 4 seconds. Traffic is heavily skewed toward developer getting-started content, with direct traffic and Google as the dominant acquisition channels. The most recent 7-day period shows a slight improvement in engagement metrics compared to the broader 14-day and 30-day windows.

Weekday vs. Weekend Traffic Drop

Visitors dropped sharply on weekends: April 11 (Sat) had 582 visitors and April 12 (Sun) had 570 — roughly half of the weekday peak of 1,127 on April 9 (Wed). This confirms a professional/developer audience driving weekday usage.

Getting-Started Funnel Dominates

The top 8 pages by pageviews are almost entirely within /guides/developer/getting-started/, indicating new developer onboarding is the primary use case. The sequential path (install → configure → get-coins → hello-world) is clearly evident.

AI Chatbot Referrals Emerging

ChatGPT (48 visitors), Gemini (13), and Claude (8) collectively sent ~69 visitors this week. While still a small share, AI assistants are becoming a measurable referral channel, with Claude users showing exceptional engagement (395s avg duration).

Improved 7-Day Engagement vs. 14-Day Baseline

Views per visit rose from 2.47 (14d) to 2.7 (7d) and avg duration from 159s to 184s, suggesting a higher-quality or more intent-driven audience in the most recent week compared to the prior week.

DeepBook and gRPC as Emerging Interest Areas

Both /standards/deepbook (140 visitors, 17% bounce) and /concepts/data-access/grpc (134 visitors, 24% bounce) show unusually low bounce rates, signaling strong topical intent. deepbook.tech is also a meaningful referral source (89 visitors), reinforcing this trend.
