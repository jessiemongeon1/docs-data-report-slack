Traffic Overview

Over the 7-day period (Apr 10–16, 2026), docs.sui.io attracted 4,553 unique visitors generating 14,845 pageviews with a 63% bounce rate, averaging 2.86 views/visit and 192 seconds on site. Traffic is heavily concentrated around getting-started and installation content, with organic search and direct traffic as the dominant acquisition channels. The 7-day window shows meaningfully stronger engagement (2.86 views/visit, 192s) compared to the 30-day baseline (2.62 views/visit, 168s), suggesting a recent uptick in visitor quality.

Peak traffic on Monday Apr 10 with mid-week recovery

Apr 10 saw the highest pageviews (2,857) and visitors (822) of the week. Traffic dipped Sat–Sun (Apr 12: 570 visitors) then recovered Mon–Wed (Apr 13–15: 662–704 visitors), confirming a weekday-driven developer audience pattern.

Getting-started funnel dominates content consumption

The top 10 pages are heavily skewed toward installation, configuration, and onboarding content. The sequential flow (install → configure client → get coins → get address → hello world) is clearly the primary user journey, representing the core developer activation path.

AI chatbot referrals emerging as a distinct channel

ChatGPT (44 visitors) and Claude (10 visitors) together sent 54 visitors — comparable to GitHub (51). Claude users show dramatically higher engagement (470s vs 121s), suggesting AI agents are becoming a meaningful but differentiated referral channel.

DeepBook content punches above its weight in engagement

The /standards/deepbook page has an 18% bounce rate and 61s scroll depth (among the best on the site), and deepbook.tech is a top referrer with 286s sessions. DeepBook developer activity is a high-engagement segment worth prioritizing in content updates.

Duplicate onboarding URLs splitting traffic

Both /guides/developer/getting-started/sui-install (616 visitors) and /getting-started/onboarding/sui-install (98 visitors) exist for similar install content. This URL fragmentation splits SEO value and user flow — consolidation or canonical tagging is recommended.

7-day engagement metrics outperform 30-day baseline

Views per visit (2.86 vs 2.62) and session duration (192s vs 168s) are both higher in the 7d window than the 30d average, suggesting a recent improvement in visitor quality or content relevance — possibly tied to a product release or content update around Apr 10.
