Traffic Overview

Over the 7-day period (Apr 10–16, 2026), docs.sui.io attracted 4,553 visitors generating 14,845 pageviews with a 63% bounce rate, 2.86 views per visit, and an average session duration of 192 seconds. Traffic is dominated by getting-started and installation content, with direct traffic and Google as the top acquisition channels. Week-over-week comparison (7d vs prior 7d from the 14d window) suggests the first half of the 14-day window had ~5,089 visitors, meaning the recent 7 days saw a ~11% traffic decline. Engagement metrics improved in the last 7 days (2.86 views/visit vs 2.49 over 14d), indicating users who do arrive are browsing more pages per session.

Peak traffic on Monday Apr 10 followed by a mid-week trough

Apr 10 saw the highest single-day traffic (822 visitors, 2,857 pageviews), then dropped sharply to ~570-582 on the weekend (Apr 11-12), recovering mid-week. This weekday-heavy pattern is typical of developer documentation audiences.

AI chatbot referrals are an emerging traffic source

ChatGPT (44 visitors) and Claude (10 visitors) together drove 54 visitors in 7 days. Claude users show exceptional engagement (470s avg duration), suggesting AI agents are actively recommending Sui docs and users arrive with high intent.

Getting-started funnel dominates page traffic

The top 10 pages are heavily concentrated in /guides/developer/getting-started/* — install, configure, get-coins, get-address, and hello-world collectively account for a major share of pageviews, confirming new developer onboarding is the primary use case.

Duplicate onboarding URLs may be fragmenting traffic

Both /guides/developer/getting-started/sui-install (616 visitors) and /getting-started/onboarding/sui-install (98 visitors) cover similar content, suggesting a URL migration with residual traffic on old paths — consolidation or canonical tags could improve SEO.

DeepBook ecosystem driving quality referrals

deepbook.tech sent 87 visitors with a low 48% bounce rate and 286s duration — among the best engagement metrics of any referral source. This indicates DeepBook developers are active, returning users of Sui docs, warranting dedicated DeepBook documentation investment.

7-day engagement metrics outperform 14d and 30d averages

Views per visit (2.86) and visit duration (192s) in the last 7 days are the highest across all measurement windows, suggesting a recent shift toward more engaged users — possibly driven by a specific event, announcement, or content update around Apr 10.
