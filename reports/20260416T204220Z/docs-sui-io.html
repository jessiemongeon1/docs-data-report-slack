Plausible

Traffic Overview

Over the 7-day period (Apr 9–15, 2026), docs.sui.io attracted 5,051 visitors and 15,408 pageviews with a 64% bounce rate, 2.7 views per visit, and an average visit duration of 184 seconds. Traffic is heavily developer-focused, with getting-started and installation guides dominating top pages. The week shows a notable traffic spike on Apr 9 (1,127 visitors) followed by a mid-week dip on the weekend (Apr 11–12) and a partial recovery. Compared to the prior 7-day period, the recent week accounts for ~51% of the 14-day total visitors (5,051 of 9,830), suggesting relatively stable week-over-week traffic with a slight improvement in engagement (2.7 vs 2.47 views/visit, 184 vs 134s duration).

Weekday vs. Weekend Traffic Drop

Visitors dropped sharply from 822 on Apr 10 (Thursday) to 570–582 on Apr 11–12 (Sat–Sun), a ~30% weekend decline, consistent with a developer-focused audience. Traffic recovered to 699 by Monday Apr 13.

Apr 9 Traffic Spike

Apr 9 was the highest-traffic day with 1,127 visitors and 2,685 pageviews, possibly driven by a content release, announcement, or external link. This is 37% above the weekly daily average (~721).

Improving Engagement Week-over-Week

The most recent 7 days show higher views/visit (2.7 vs 2.47) and longer session duration (184s vs 159s) compared to the full 14-day window, suggesting the second week's cohort is more engaged than the prior week.

AI Chatbot Referrals Emerging

ChatGPT (48 visitors), Claude (8), and Gemini (13) together account for ~69 visitors this week. Claude sessions average 395s — the highest engagement of any referral source — suggesting AI-referred users have strong research intent.

Getting-Started Funnel Dominates Content

Six of the top 10 pages are in the /guides/developer/getting-started/ path, confirming new developer onboarding is the primary use case. The install → configure → get-coins → hello-world flow is clearly the dominant journey.
