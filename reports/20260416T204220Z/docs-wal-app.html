Plausible

Traffic Overview

docs.wal.app received 11,795 unique visitors and 18,625 pageviews over the 7-day period (Apr 9–15, 2026). Traffic grew steadily across the week, with the homepage and Walrus Sites intro pages dominating. The vast majority of traffic (95%+) is direct, with minimal referral diversity. Engagement metrics are modest — 82% bounce rate, 38-second average visit duration — suggesting most visitors are quick-reference seekers.

Consistent weekday-to-weekend traffic

Traffic dipped on Apr 10–11 (Thu–Fri: 1,222–1,381 visitors) then rebounded strongly Mon–Tue (Apr 14–15: 2,016–2,142 visitors), suggesting a primarily weekday professional audience.

Improving daily trajectory

Daily visitors grew from 1,880 on Apr 9 to 2,142 on Apr 15 — a 14% increase over the week — indicating sustained or growing momentum rather than a one-time spike.

Walrus Sites content is a major engagement driver

The /walrus-sites/intro pages collectively rival the homepage in raw pageviews and vastly outperform it in scroll depth (100% vs 10%), making Walrus Sites the most compelling content area.

Recent traffic quality lower than 30d baseline

The 7d bounce rate (82%) and views/visit (1.36) are worse than the 30d figures (67%, 1.62), suggesting the recent visitor cohort is less exploratory — possibly driven by direct-link sharing or broader awareness campaigns.

Kapa

Developer Pain Point Themes

This week's 63 Walrus conversations centered on blob storage mechanics, mainnet onboarding friction, and blob/object ID confusion. Key pain points include CLI setup, blob enumeration limitations, and token management workflows.

#1 13 conversations high priority

Blob Storage Methods and Write Interfaces (CLI, HTTP API, SDK)

Developers cannot confidently store or retrieve blobs without comparing three underdocumented interfaces.

#2 9 conversations high priority

Mainnet Setup and CLI Onboarding

First-time mainnet friction at key import and wallet funding blocks developers before any meaningful usage.

#3 9 conversations high priority

Global Blob Enumeration and Indexing Limitations

Users hit hard dead ends with no documented workaround, eroding trust in the platform's observability.

#4 9 conversations high priority

Blob ID and Sui Object ID Relationship

ID confusion causes failed retrievals and wasted debugging time for developers bridging Sui and Walrus.

#5 6 conversations medium priority

Token Management and Coin Operations (WAL/SUI)

Missing CLI examples for coin merging and WAL transfer block users from completing basic transactions.

#6 5 conversations medium priority

Epoch System and Timing

Without epoch duration reference, developers cannot reliably plan blob storage and renewal windows.
