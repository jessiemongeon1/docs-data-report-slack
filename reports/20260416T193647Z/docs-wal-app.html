Plausible

Traffic Overview

Over the 7-day period (Apr 9–15, 2026), docs.wal.app attracted 11,795 unique visitors and 18,625 pageviews, with a high bounce rate of 82% and a short average visit duration of 38 seconds. Traffic is heavily dominated by direct visits. Compared to the prior 7-day window (implied from the 14d total of 28,580 visitors), the most recent week accounts for ~41% of the 14-day total, suggesting relatively stable but slightly declining traffic in the earlier week. The 30-day view shows much stronger engagement (bounce rate 67%, 1.62 views/visit), indicating that recent traffic quality has dipped.

Traffic Recovering and Growing Through the Week

Daily visitors rose from 1,222 on Apr 10 to 2,142 on Apr 15, a 75% increase over 6 days. If this momentum continues, next week could see stronger overall numbers.

Recent Engagement Drop vs. 30-Day Baseline

The 7-day bounce rate (82%) and views/visit (1.36) are notably worse than the 30-day averages (67%, 1.62), suggesting a recent influx of lower-intent traffic or a change in traffic sources.

Walrus Sites Documentation Is the Top Content Area

The /walrus-sites/* pages (intro + tutorial) collectively account for a disproportionate share of pageviews, indicating strong developer interest in the Walrus Sites product specifically.

AI Referral Traffic Is Small but High Quality

ChatGPT-referred users (10 visitors, 161s duration) outperform Google-referred users (203 visitors, 53s duration) by 3x on engagement. Monitoring AI referral growth is worthwhile as this channel scales.

Legacy .html URLs Still Receiving Significant Traffic

Pages like /walrus-sites/intro.html and /walrus-sites/tutorial.html each receive ~2,100 and ~23 visitors respectively, matching their non-.html counterparts. These should be canonicalized or redirected to avoid split metrics.
